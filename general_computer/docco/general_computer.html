Introduction

This is a working model of what was a thought-experiment discussed in Appendix C of The Manual v2_Beta_04. It was a thought experiment then as there were limitations in the trad4 infrastructure that meant it could not be built. Those limitations have now been fixed and the model is presented here.

The plan is to create a series of objects that perform primitive mathematical functions â€“ plus, minus, natural_log, cume_norm_dist etc., and configure the relationship between these objects to solve a particular problem. The interesting thing about this approach is that once we've created the application, we can configure the relationship between the objects at run-time by manipulating data in the database.

Throughout this document we'll be referring to the data set "bs_delta" which will solve for delta in the closed-form Black-Scholes for European call options on non-dividend paying stocks. This is given by: 

delta = N(d1)

where
	d1 = (ln(S/K) + rT + vvT/2) / v * sqrt(T)
and

	N(x) is the cumulative normal distribution of x
and
	S = stock price
	K = strike price
	r = risk free rate
	v = volatility
	vv = volatility squared
	T = time to maturity
It doesn't matter if you're not familiar with this equation - it's the arithmatic itself we're interested in - not what any of it actually means.

Inheritance of Interface (IoI)

Inheitance of interface was introduced to allow for this model to be built.

The Model

First we start by identifying the operations we'll need. Looking at the equation above this give us

  • addition
  • multiplication
  • division
  • natural_log
  • square_root
  • cume_norm_dist

For completeness, we may as well add subtraction as while it's not used in the above equation it may be used in other configurations.
